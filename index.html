Express Entry

Express Entry is a pathway for skilled workers in Canada or overseas who want to immigrate to Canada permanently. This program selects skilled workers based on their skills and ability to contribute to Canada’s economy. This program manages three different federal economic immigration categories.

Provincial Nominee

This program is for workers with the skills, education and work experience to contribute to the economy of a specific province or territory where they want to live and become permanent residents of Canada.

Rural and Northern Immigration Pilot

The Rural and Northern Immigration Pilot is designed to help participating communities in Canada's rural and remote areas attract and retain skilled workers. This pilot is community-driven, which means the community will assess prospective candidates who best fit the community’s economic needs and recommend candidates for permanent residence to IRCC for a final decision.

Family Sponsorship

Family Sponsorship allows Canadian citizens and Permanent Residents to sponsor their family members to immigrate permanently to Canada. You are eligible to sponsor if you are at least 18 years old, a Canadian citizen or permanent resident, financially capable of supporting your sponsored family member and not receiving social assistance except for disability. The family member you can sponsor includes your spouse, common-law partner, conjugal partner and dependent children.

Caregivers

Caregivers can obtain Permanent Residency in Canada through the Home Child Care Provider Pilot and the Home Support Worker Pilot. This is a 5-year pilot program that has a cap and resets every year. The pilot program allows qualified caregivers and their family members to come to Canada with the intention of becoming permanent residents

Atlantic Immigration Program

This program is for skilled foreign workers and international graduates who want to work and immigrate in 1 of Canada’s 4 Atlantic provinces ( Nova Scotia, New Brunswick, Prince Edward Island, Newfoundland and Labrador).

Temporary Resident Visas

Supervisa is for parents and grandparents who want to visit their children or grandchildren in Canada for an extended period. With this visa, the validity is up to 10 years, allowing multiple visits for up to 5 years at a time.

Visitor Visa

Whether you're coming to Canada for tourism, business, or visiting family and friends, you need to have a visitor visa to enter Canada. (Some countries do not require a visitor visa but need an Electronic Travel Authorization (ETA). Normally, most visitors are allowed to stay up to 6 months depending on the purpose of the visit.
